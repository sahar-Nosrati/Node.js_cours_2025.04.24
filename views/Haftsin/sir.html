Haft-Seen: A Symbolic Celebration of Renewal

As part of the Nowruz (Iranian New Year) celebration, the Haft-Seen table is a cherished and meaningful tradition that honors the arrival of spring and the rebirth of nature. “Haft-Seen” means “Seven S’s,” referring to seven symbolic items that all begin with the Persian letter س (Seen). Each item on the table carries a hopeful wish for the new year. Common elements include: Sabzeh (sprouted greens) for rebirth and growth Senjed (dried oleaster fruit) for love, Seer(garlic) for health, Sib (apple) for beauty and vitality, Samanu (sweet wheat pudding) for strength and power, Serkeh (vinegar) for patience and wisdom, Sekkeh (coins) for wealth and prosperity The Haft-Seen is often accompanied by other meaningful items, such as a mirror, candles, painted eggs, poetry books, a goldfish, and sometimes a holy book—each adding layers of symbolism, spirituality, and joy. Together, these elements create more than just a festive display. The Haft-Seen table reflects the values of hope, renewal, light, and balance—a beautiful echo of the spring equinox and the spirit of new beginnings.
